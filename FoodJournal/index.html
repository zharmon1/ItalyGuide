La Firenze

May 24, 2023 (Lunch)

Panetteria e Stuzzicheria De Neri in Florence, Italy

The La Firenze is a sandwich consisting of Tuscan ham, Tuscan pecorino, truffle cream, fresh tomatoes, and rocket salad. It was a very large sandwich for the price and was extremely filling. The bread was crispy and dry, but the rest of the ingredients made up for it. The flavors of the ham, cheese, and cream came together and blended surprisingly well and unless you really tried, it was difficult to distinguish between the tastes of the different ingredients. In all, it made for a great lunch that wasn't too small but was still light.

Lasagne Tradizionali al Forno

May 24, 2023 (Dinner)

Ristorante Boccadama in Florence, Italy

The lasagna at Ristorante Boccadama is their self proclaimed house specialty and it definitely does not disappoint. The pasta was extremely tender and had a melt in your mouth texture that paired perfectly with the flavor. The lasagna was served at the perfect temperature and was clearly made to order. The beef used also had a good flavor and could've stood as a solid dish without the pasta. The only downside was that it did not pair well with the house wine, however, that is more of a knock on the wine as it was not the greatest itself. I would definitely recommend this meal as a dinner option.

Quattro Stagioni Pizze Rosse

May 25, 2023 (Lunch)

Pizzeria Toto in Florence, Italy

The Quattro Stagioni pizza consisted of cooked ham, mushrooms, artichokes, and olives on tomato sauce with some mozzarella cheese on top. The name of the pizza, which means "four seasons" in English, is very telling of the type of dish this pizza is because each topping makes the pizza feel entirely different. Also, each topping had mostly its own slice so it was almost like eating a slice of four separate pizzas. The ham slice had the familiar and almost American-style pizza taste. The mushroom was a bit softer but gave its slice a meat-like flavor. The artichoke slice was full of artichokes that burst in your mouth. These sudden bursts of flavor did not pair well with the pizza and ultimately, I do not believe artichoke should be a pizza topping. Finally, the olive slice was surprisingly tasty. The colder, sweeter olives paired well with the pizza. In all, the pizza made for a decent lunch, however, I do not believe it was good when compared to other pizzas.

Hamburger Rione

May 25, 2023 (Dinner)

Rione Brewpub Firenze in Florence, Italy

The Hamburger Rione was an absolutely amazing burger. It was the house burger of the American style bar and it did not disappoint. The burger was easily the size of 2 and was topped with triplo pane, pecorino, insalata, pomodoro, chedar, due anelli di cipolla fritti, bacon, and salsa della casa. All of these flavors blended together excellently and were the perfect taste of home with an Italian twist. It also paired amazingly with their Hildegard beer. The burger was cooked between medium and medium well which made it perfectly tender. It is definitely a must try for any hungry traveler.

Big Mac

May 26, 2023 (Lunch)

McDonald's Firenze Esterno Stazione in Florence, Italy

Despite being an ocean away from America, this Big Mac tasted exactly like any other Big Mac from an American McDonald's. Even the french fries and Sprite I had with it were unchanged. The only noticeable difference was that the burger patty was a normal beef color instead of the typical greyish brown you see in America. Overall, the taste and texture were that of your typical cheap McDonald's Big Mac and there is not much else to note. It's a great option for anyone feeling a bit homesick.

Bistecca Alla Florentina

May 26, 2023 (Dinner)

Osteria Del Cinghiale Bianco in Florence, Italy

The Florentine steak is a classic dish famous in Florence and it does not disappoint. The steak I had was cooked medium rare (the standard), but it had the tenderness of a medium or medium-well steak without the chewiness of a rarer steak. The salt crystals sprinkled about gave some bites more flavor and a slightly different texture than the rest because the crystals were relatively large. The steak was seasoned in a way that compliments the typical meat taste instead of attempting to drown it out, which is a mistake many smaller steakhouses make in America. The steak was also massive (0.7 - 1.0 kg) and could easily feed two. Overall, it was a nice meal. Very well-suited for a fancier night out or possibly a date as long as you are comfortable with the bloodiness of a rarer steak.

Tacchino

May 27, 2023 (Lunch)

La Terrazza Cigolini in Monterosso, Italy

The tacchino is your basic turkey sandwich; complete with mayo, lettuce, and tomato. As you can see from the picture, the bread has an interesting design that almost looks like a large slice of swiss cheese. The sandwich was server cold and if you have ever had a turkey sandwich, you've had this sandwich. The lemonade paired well with it and was actually the most interesting part of the meal. The lemonade was not like American lemonade, but it was actually more like a fresh squeezed lemon juice with extra pulp. Extremely sour, yet also very satisfying and thirst quenching in the midday heat.

Rustica

May 27, 2023 (Dinner)

Lapo's Restaurant, Pizza & Drink in Monterosso, Italy

The Rustica pizza was an amazing pizza topped with tomato, mozzarella, sausage, and onion. As you can tell, this pizza is closer in style to a typical American pizza than the previous one mentioned. I am typically not a huge fan of onion, however, onion definitely belonged on this pizza because the pairing of the tastes of the onion and the sausage was nearly perfect. In addition, the evenly dispersed cheese was perfectly milky, and chunks of tomato made each bite unique, which is exactly what Italian dishes attempt to be. It perfectly accomplishes the task of making an Americanized dish in the Italian style for all the hungry tourist who had just had a long beach day.

Hotdog

May 28, 2023 (Lunch)

Food Stand in Pisa, Italy

This hotdog was far from that standard "American" hotdog. In every sense it seemed like someone who never has never seen a hotdog went and made one from a verbal description. The hotdog was served on a sub-style piece of bread. The hotdog itself was cut in half and laid on the bread. Then, the ketchup was applied like a condiment would be on a sub. Outside of the hotdog being barely warm, the bread being slightly stale, and the ketchup drowning any chance of taste; it was a decent lunch. Nothing to write home about, but still alright. The taste was mostly just ketchup and any small taste of hotdog can be described as the taste of any precooked frozen hotdog. A great lunch for the tourist heavy environment around the Leaning Tower area. You definitely get what you pay for.

Spaghettoni Trafilati Al Bronzo Con Cozze E Datterini Su Crema Di Patate

May 28, 2023 (Dinner)

Tamerò in Florence, Italy

This spaghettoni was the most interesting and different dish I've had so far. It is exclusively on the Spring-Summer menu at Tamerò and it was unlike any seafood dish I've had before. Instead of your traditional meat sauce, the spaghettoni was made with a mussel sauce. This gave the dish a more fishy smell than the pasta I am used to. It also had an almost shrimp like taste, however, that is all I can describe it as because I am not familiar with the taste of mussels. The spaghettoni was quite thick, so it did not retain its sauce as well as you would like when it was picked up with a fork. The texture of the dish was slightly more chewy than a spaghetti with meat sauce. This was partially due to the larger type of pasta and partially due to the use of seafood. Overall, it was a decent dish that anyone should try at least once, but personally, I will stick to land based meats in my pasta.

Lasagna

May 29, 2023 (Lunch)

Café Deja Vu in Fiesole, Italy

This lasagna was nothing like a hand made lasagna. It was a bit dry, not nearly as creamy as others I've had, and overall tasted like a frozen lasagna that had been reheated in the oven. The dish lacked much cheese that wasn't baked on and it was surprisingly difficult to cut with a fork. Despite all this, it had a good flavor and texture that is very reminiscent of a "fast food" style lasagna that you would find at a place like Fazoli's. With it having such a cheap price point, it was definitely worth its cost and is a good lunch option for a quick and cheap meal when you are out visiting the ruins in Fiesole.

Lasagna and Tagliatelle

May 29, 2023 (Dinner)

Pasta Class in Florence, Italy

During our pasta class we created our own lasagna and tagliatelle by hand that we would later get to eat, and the freshness showed itself. Both dishes were extremely tender and had the melt in your mouth feel that anyone would want in a pasta. The ragu and cheese blended together amazingly to create a lasagna with a perfect creamy filling between each layer of fresh pasta. The tagliatelle was the perfect side dish. The longer and thicker noodles of the tagliatelle offered a simpler flavor palate when compared to the lasagna, which was the perfect combination for a meal in Italy. I may be a little biased since I made it, but I believe this may be the best lasagna I've had and maybe will ever have in Italy.

Panino Di Manzo Su Schiacciate

May 30, 2023 (Lunch)

Piccadilly in Florence, Italy

This sandwich from Piccadilly is quite a mysterious sandwich. On the menu is is labeled as "Schiacciate" which is just the type of bread it is served on and the meat on the sandwich could easily be either roast beef or lampredotto (meat from a cow stomach). I am leaning towards the latter, however, it was a good meal either way. The sandwich was extremely simple, consisting of only bread and the meat. The bread was toasted to the point of near inedibility and tasted very similar to a white bread toast that was just extremely tough to bite into. The meat, on the other hand, had an extremely good taste and texture. Its taste had a slight spice to it, almost as if it had been marinating prior to being cooked and it had the texture of a higher quality lunch meat that wasn't chewy or thin but is still tender. If you were to remove the bread from the sandwich and eat the meat on its own or if the bread were just not toasted, it could have been one of the better sandwiches I've had in town. Regardless, it still made for a decent lunch when paired with an Aperol Spritz.

Crispy Golocious

May 30, 2023 (Dinner)

Golocious Burger & Wine in Florence, Italy

The Crispy Golocious burger was a 200g meat patty with classic cheddar cheese, white cheddar cheese, bacon, and their "Crispy" sauce. To put it simply, it was one of the best burgers I've had. Each topping/condiment worked to compliment the flavor of the meat instead of do its own thing. The meat itself was your classic beef patty seasoned with something similar to a steak's seasoning. The meat was clearly never frozen and it was noticeable that they took their time cooking it instead of rushing it because the patty was perfectly tender and was neither tough nor chewy. The cheeses were stringy when bitten into which gave the burger a unique eating experience. The bacon was also perfect as it gave the burger patty a greasier, fattier counterpart to contribute to each bite. Finally, the "Crispy" sauce was one of the best house sauces I've tried. It is comparable to In-N-Out's famous burger sauce in taste and similarly, the "Crispy" sauce works to add a slight sweetness to the burger and does not oversaturate the overall taste. Overall, this burger was amazing and is the perfect break from the constant meals of pasta.

Club Sandwich

May 31, 2023 (Lunch)

Sfarinà in Bologna, Italy

This sandwich was from a small shop at one of Bologna's farmers markets. It consisted of grilled chicken, bacon, lettuce, tomato, and mustard mayonnaise on a folded piece of flatbread. Overall, it was a solid light lunch. The chicken was tender and not chewy. The bacon had an odd amount of spicy to it that really helped the sandwich taste complete. The veggies and condiment gave the sandwich more definition, made it less dry, and gave it a much healthier feeling. The biggest plus was the bread. It was an extremely nice break from the hard, terrible Tuscan bread. This bread was soft with a simple taste similar to the Artisan Flatbread at Subway. The only real downside to the sandwich was that it wasn't very filling.

Chicken Tenders and Mashed Potatoes

May 31, 2023 (Dinner)

KFC in Bologna, Italy

The 3 piece chicken tenders with mashed potatoes and gravy from Kentucky Fried Chicken in the Bologna train station was exactly the taste of home I needed. The chicken was crispy and easy to tear apart. It tasted like the KFC chicken at home, but it was far less greasy. The potatoes here were much better than the bland, cardboard potatoes back home. You could tell these were real potatoes that were nearly fresh because they did not need the gravy to have a taste. Overall, it was a great meal that was also cheap, quick, and not pasta. Plus they had free refills!

Max Cheese Burger

June 01, 2023 (Dinner)

K2 Royal Chicken in Florence, Italy

You can only eat so much pasta, so I tried an imitation American fast food joint. The burger consisted of 3 patties, cheese, lettuce, pickle, tomato, ketchup, mayonnaise, and barbeque sauce all on a sesame seed bun. Both the burger and fries are made fresh when you order, so the food actually tastes and presents as very high quality compared to true American places like McDonald's. The burger tastes very good. The flavors blend very nicely, and I am a huge fan of how the BBQ adds a bit of tanginess to each bit and there is a noticeable lack of grease. Other than that, it is a standard fast food burger.

Ravioli in Crema di Tartufo e Pecorino

Alluvione Pizza

June 03, 2023 (Dinner)

Antico Capon Pizzeria

This pizza was the best I've had in Italy so far. It was topped with tomato, mozzarella, spicy salami, Vienna sausage, Soppressa ham, and mushrooms. The amount of meat and cheese on this pizza made it taste almost like an American pizza (which I believe is superior so far). The crust was difficult to eat in the sections without toppings because it was burnt, dry, and tough. The salami was difficult to cut and just tore since I had only a dull butter knife. Outside of these small issues, it was an amazing pizza. It was not too chewy nor did it fall apart very often, so I can't complain at all. The taste was that of a typical meat lover's pizza.

Spaghetti Al Ragu

June 04, 2023 (Dinner)

Osteria Del Cinghiale Bianco in Florence, Italy

The spaghetti al ragu was amazing. It was my second time at Osteria Del Cinghiale Bianco, but my first getting spaghetti in Italy. The noodles were perfect. They had a typical noodle taste and texture but didn't feel too short as spaghetti in America often does when you try to twirl it on a fork. The ragu was the cherry on top. It was a perfect consistency for it to stick to the pasta while still having enough meat in each clump to satisfy my taste buds. Also it smelt as good as it tasted!

Chicken Nuggets

June 05, 2023 (Lunch)

K2 Royal Chicken in Florence, Italy

These chicken nuggets were simply great. They were extremely meaty with a perfect thin, crispy breading. The meat was white meat that was not a mash up of leftover parts, but rather small parts of what was likely chicken breasts. They had the perfect greasy, fried taste of your typical chicken nuggets. It was very similar in taste to a chicken nugget from Cook Out, but it had more meat in it. Plus, they were fresh made so they were warm and the perfect light lunch for someone craving something fried.

Salsiccia Calzoni

June 05, 2023 (Dinner)

La Grotta Di Leo in Florence, Italy

This was my first calzone and it did not disappoint. The first thing to point out is that the only tomato sauce and olive oil present in the dish is what is visible in the image. The inside was filled solely with cheese and sausage. The crust was perfectly soft, which made for an easy meal to eat, but it did not fall apart. Often the breads here are either too stale to bite through or so soft that they fall apart with the toppings, so this was a much needed experience. The texture was slightly mushy in some spots, but similar to a bread loaf with cheese on it in others. It tasted amazing. The cheeses came together with the spice of the sausage to make each bite an explosion of flavor inside of a piece of bread. Overall, it was amazing and I will definitely be trying calzones again.

Mushroom Pizza

June 06, 2023 (Lunch)

Alice Pizza Tivoli in Tivoli, Italy

This was our quick meal before our trip in Tivoli, and it did the job but wasn't amazing. I should add a disclaimer that I am not the largest mushroom fan, but this was all that was available within the time frame needed that looked decent. With that being said, it wasn't bad. The mushrooms added a decent flavor at times, but tasted burnt at other times. The meal as a whole was less pizza and more of a Cheesy Bread from Domino's that was made with mushrooms on top. It was 90% bread with a small amount of cheese and mushroom. When you could taste anything other than bread, it was pretty good.

BBQ Chicken Milf

June 06, 2023 (Dinner)

Keb Hub Roma Pepita's in Rome, Italy

This was a very odd restaurant to say the least (it was food porn themed with a regular porn style), however, the food was surprisingly good. The BBQ Chicken Milf was a bunch of pressed chicken nuggets covered in BBQ that are wrapped in a flatbread. The flatbread taste was very similar to a waffle while its texture and flexibility was similar to Subway's Artisan Flatbread. The chicken was very good. They were your typical boneless BBQ chicken wings, but with a lack of BBQ and chicken in my diet lately, it was a nice feeling to eat. When eaten as a whole, the chicken overtakes the taste of the bread, which I believe is good, but could be bad depending on the person. As a whole, it was a great meal after a long day of travel.

Cheeseburger di Manzo

June 07, 2023 (Dinner)

Rione 13 Ristorante in Rome, Italy

Continuing the streak, this was an amazing burger. It consisted of a beef patty on a homemade bun with tomato, lettuce, cheddar, and mozzarella cheese. It was also served with fries on a shovel instead of a plate, which was a nice touch. The burger was large, smelt like any good burger, and the toppings all came together to form an amazing taste. The thing that really stood out, though, was the texture of the beef patty. It felt like pulled pork with the taste of beef instead of the typical ground beef patty texture. Say Cheeeeeese Burger

June 08, 2023 (Dinner)

Teddy’s Lab - Trastevere in Rome, Italy

I don't think that there is a bad burger in Italy. The Say Cheeeeeese Burger at Teddy's Lab consists of a 200g Danish beef smash burger on a homemade bun with double cheddar, bacon, and Teddy sauce. It was one of the best smash burgers I've ever had. The bun is soft, but doesn't get soggy. The patty is that perfect fresh grilled burger flavor, and it is tender without being chewy or fatty. The Teddy sauce is amazing, but I have absolutely no clue what is in it or even a guess as to what it could be. The sauce's flavor works so well with the cheese and bacon that it is honestly nearly impossible to separate it out in order to describe its flavor in my opinion. It was amazing and I will definitely be going back.

Frittura Di Calamarie Gamberi

June 09, 2023 (Lunch)

Hostaria Al Gladiatore in Rome, Italy

I decided to be a bit more adventurous for lunch and was not disappointed. This dish consisted of fried squid, fried prawns, a couple fried baby octopus (which the menu did not mention), and some lettuce to pretty it up. The fried calamari was amazing. The greasiness perfectly complemented the flavor making what tasted like a slightly chewier and fishier chicken ring. The prawns were also pretty good. They were full without the intestines removed, so that was a little off putting, but the taste was still superb and identical to any fried shrimp. Finally, the octopus was not my cup of tea. The tentacles were simply too chewy and slimy when you swallowed for me. Plus, I was getting full anyways. Overall, it made for a decent lunch and I'm glad I ventured out even if I don't intend to eat it again.

Texas BBQ Carnitas Burrito

June 09, 2023 (Dinner)

Pico's Taqueria & American Grill in Rome, Italy

I did not expect to have one of the best burritos I've ever eaten in Italy, but I did. This dish was cheese, basmati rice, jalapenos, black beans, and BBQ pulled pork all wrapped up in a tortilla. It looked and smelt amazing and, luckily, it also had a taste to match. The way the cheese seemed to allow the rest of the ingredients to melt in your mouth, while not overtalking all of the other flavors was simply perfect. Each bite you could individually taste each ingredient, but they also came together to form the perfect Tex-Mex meal. The BBQ on the pork was slightly tangy which only further helped this burrito. I will definitely have this meal again before I leave.

Dirty Donts Burger

June 10, 2023 (Dinner)

Donts in Rome, Italy

As you probably can't tell from the picture, this was an extremely good smash burger. It was 2 patties with cheese, bacon, fries, mayo, and Donts sauce. As you can probably tell since this food journal is turning into a burger journal, the burgers in Italy are amazing, and this is no exception. The sweet mayo pairs perfectly with the tangy Donts sauce. The fries gave a softness that paired with the bacon's crunchiness. Then the cheese with the smash burger gave the classic fresh grilled cheeseburger taste. Every ingredient had a match, making an amazing burger. It smelt like any cheeseburger would, but the offsetting fries and bacon caused the texture to seem as if neither was there at times despite being able to taste them. Overall, an amazing experience and if Teddy's Lab wasn't better, faster, and cheaper; I would go here more often.

BBQ Burger

June 13, 2023 (Lunch)

Bandidos Hamburger in Rome, Italy

If you expect me to say that this burger was amazing, you are correct. The burger consisted of a 160g patty with lettuce, tomato, onion rings, and BBQ sauce.
