Identify a work or art and craft a post that describes all that you know about the subject.

Giovanni Maria Butteri’s “Coronation of the Virgin” was a work of art that stood out to me. It hangs in the sacistry of the Basilica di Santo Spirito alongside Michelangelo’s Crucifix. It stood out to me because this seemingly typical painting was placed with many extremely important artifacts yet it was unnamed in the basilica. In fact, the painting was only restored in November 2022 and as far as I can find, has no formal title. So it stood out to me that this painting would be with Michelangelo’s Crucifix and bones of saints, so I took a closer look. I noticed that there are many small details that all are nothing at first glance, however, they are all important ideas in Christian lifestyles. For starters, the obvious pick is God is depicted with a book with an Alpha and Omega in it, referring to Him calling Himself the Alpha and Omega in the book of Revelation. Next, Jesus and God are depicted on the same level, showing that they are one and the same. Another point is that the dove which symbolizes salvation, peace, and overall goodness is seen above Mary as she is crowned showing us that these things are true of those in heaven. The final detail is easily overlooked and even often cropped out of online pictures. At the bottom of the painting there are two women. One is picking up a cross and one is kneeling over a vase. This depicts a divide in humanity as the girl with the cross is visually performing the Christian act of “picking up your cross” and following Jesus as well as wearing a crown of thorns like Him and she is even shown to be looking up at Him. The girl with the vase seems to be showing the more materialistic side of humanity which is obsessed with “desires of the flesh” and she is shown to be looking away from God. In total these details of this one innocent painting convey the basic beliefs of Christianity as a whole, and these are just the ones I saw and that really stood out to me. I believe this says a lot about Florence and the Florentine culture because if you look closely enough at every small detail you find, it is likely that you will be able to find the Christian influence that shaped the city.

This was the question and answer for a discusion board for May 24, 2023. The discusion title was "What aspect of Florence have really piqued your curiosity?" I chose this discusion because it is over a work of art that I saw that seemed to have a ton of hidden meaning. Despite this, there are not many people who have discussed it online, and it was actually difficult to find without knowing the title and only the painter. This caused me to be very interested in it, so I wrote about it on this discussion post.

Florentine Roofs by Zach Harmon

Glistening in the sun you sit still as could be.

Atop the bustling streets, oddly it's you that is often overlooked.

They say you are for their protection, but I know you are much more.

When I look across the city, you are the first thing that I see.

Your red clay tiles are nothing short of historied.


For years you’ve sat never-changing.

While the world around you does nothing but.

You are testament to your time

Through years of rain and thunder banging,

you’ve protected every man, woman, and mutt.


Each night I step to the balcony and see you.

Encapsulated by your beauty, I stare.

You are a keeper of history and our protector at once.

To you, we have so many debts due.

Oh, Florentine roofs, to you there is nothing to compare.

This poem was written for the first project, which was to bring attention to something that you've noticed in Florence that is abstract or may be overlooked. I decided to write about the beautiful red roofs that span the city. Sure, you may notice them at first, but over time you begin to overlook them and take them for granted. So, this ode is for them. All of the red roofs that have beared the weather for us and have kept us safe.
